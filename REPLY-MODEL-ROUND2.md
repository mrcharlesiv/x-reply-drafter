## Length Analysis (BIMODAL - NOT just "be short")

- **<50 chars:** 65.4 avg likes ✅ 
- **50-100 chars:** 35.0 avg likes ❌ DEAD ZONE
- **100-200 chars:** 50.0 avg likes
- **200-400 chars:** 64.8 avg likes ✅ (ties with ultra-short)
- **400+ chars:** 34.5 avg likes ❌

**Key insight:** Go very short OR substantive (200-400). The middle ground kills engagement.

## Timing (NEW - Critical for real-world usage)

- **Within 30 min:** 101.5 avg likes ⭐ 4x better
- **30-120 min:** 58.3 avg likes
- **120+ min:** 24.9 avg likes

**Speed is ~4x more impactful than late replies.**

## Hook Types

- **Question hooks:** 189.6 avg likes ⭐ DOMINATE
- **Agreement openers ("Great point!"):** 3.2 avg likes ❌ DEAD

## Tone Rankings

- **Curious:** 171.3 avg likes ⭐ 
- **Enthusiastic:** 72.1 avg likes
- **Authoritative:** 37.0 avg likes

## Anti-Patterns (Still Apply)

- Mini-essays
- Sycophantic openers ("Great point!", "Love this", "Absolutely")
- Hedging language ("I think maybe", "Just a thought")
- Reframes ("The real question is...")
- Generic agreement
- Numbered lists restating the original

## Tactical Rules

1. **Lead with a genuine question** (121.6 avg) — not rhetorical
2. **Keep it under 50 chars OR go 200-400** — avoid middle ground
3. **Reply within 30 minutes** (101.5 avg) — timing matters enormously
4. **Be curious, not authoritative** (171.3 vs 37 avg)
5. **Don't explain, don't hedge, don't qualify**
6. **One idea per reply. One question per reply.**
